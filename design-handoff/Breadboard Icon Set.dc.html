On the canvas

The placed parts get the same treatment — cream resistor body with real colour bands, domed LED with a rim flange, D-shaped TO-92, notched DIP, and breakout modules with a header strip and gold pads.

Generic vs bespoke

Most parts share a generic body on purpose — a DIP chip really is a black chip, and an off-board module is a small PCB you wire to by pin name. Seven inline discretes were the problem: they borrowed the wrong shape entirely. Those get real bodies; everything else stays generic (off-board cards now also carry their icon).
